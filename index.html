Public Wi-Fi hotspots can be a hacker's paradise. Following these basic security tips can mean the difference between safe surfing and an ID theft or data-loss nightmare.


People are addicted to free Wi-Fi. They need it, they crave it, and they don't think twice about connecting to any network that can get them online in most cases. Getting Wi-Fi in a hotel, on an airplane, even in a restaurant or bar drives decision-making on where to go and stay. Many people even use public Wi-Fi in hotels/rentals to watch adult content—and I'm not talking about HBO Max. Yet most people can't tell a secure Wi-Fi network from an insecure one.


For many, public Wi-Fi hotspots are too convenient to ignore. But they're risky, especially because it's not that hard to make sure you're secure. Some of the tips below involve common sense; the rest you can set up before you leave the house or office. Make sure the next hotspot you connect to—be it in a café or in the sky—isn't a security nightmare waiting to happen.

Let's take a journey into setting up Apple's Vision Pro headset. We're here to guide you through the whole process.


If you've just snagged yourself an Apple Vision Pro, you're likely buzzing with excitement to dive into the virtual reality experience. And if you haven't, well, you're likely intrigued about the intricacies of setting up a $3,500 VR headset.


Before we delve into the setup drill, it's important to note that Apple exclusively sells the Vision Pro through its website or retail outlets. This is because a meticulous fitting process is necessary to ensure the headset fits snugly on your face. If you opt for online purchase, you'll need an iPhone or iPad equipped with Face ID. The Apple website will present a QR code for you to scan with your device, initiating a face scan process.
